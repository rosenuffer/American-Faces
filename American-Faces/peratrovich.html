Elizabeth Peratrovich

Elizabeth Peratrovich, a Tlingit activist, fought tirelessly for Native Alaskan rights. Her pivotal role in the passage of the Alaska Anti-Discrimination Act of 1945 challenged racial discrimination and set the stage for future civil rights movements. Peratrovich's legacy inspires us to stand up against injustice and fight for equality for all marginalized communities. You can learn more about her work here.
