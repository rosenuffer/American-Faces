Sojourner Truth

Sojourner Truth, born into slavery in 1797, emerged as a powerful advocate for abolition, women's rights, and social justice. Escaping slavery with her infant daughter, she embarked on a lifelong journey of activism and empowerment. Truth's powerful speeches, including her iconic "Ain't I a Woman?" address, which you can read here , challenged societal norms and called for an end to slavery and the oppression of women. Her indomitable spirit and commitment to equality continue to inspire generations, cementing her as an influential figure in the fight for freedom and human rights.
